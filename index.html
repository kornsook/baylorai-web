Unsupervised Deep Learning

Detail

In the wide state-of-the-art concerning non-supervised attacks, machine-learning techniques appeared about ten years ago. In particular clustering methods attracted considerable interest. Today, the deep-learning research makes clustering algorithms evolve, in particular through "embedding" techniques. These techniques aim at represent data into a space that enhances certain “useful” relations among data. The principal application domain of these techniques today is the representation of words for the natural language analysis: a useful representation should embed words into a space where words belonging to the same semantic field are close to each other. The goal of this research is studying “deep embedding” techniques, evaluating their suitability for non-profiled attack scenarios, in particular in the context of both symmetric and public key cryptographic algorithms, formalizing an efficient deep-clustering-based attack strategy and deeply analyzing its properties.

Adversarial Machine Learning

Detail

We are studying in the security aspect of machine learning, especially deep learning, during the test time. Specifically, the potential attack is a so-called adversarial attack, by which adversarial examples are carefully generated. Those examples are classified by the target classifer as a different thing from the perception of humans. Therefore, we are finding the vulnerability of deep learning in term of adversarial attacks and the countermeasures against them.

Additionally, we focuses on the utilization of adversarial training for other applications such as Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) which can generate artificial images that look realitic to humans. Recently, we also study Adversarial Neural Cryptography which utilizes the adversarial training.

NeuCrypto: Adversarial Neural Cryptography

Pablo Rivas, Javier Orduz and Korn Sooksatra
Detail

We are studying how to apply adversarial training to the field of cryptography. Inspired by this work, we aim to create an application that can encrypt and decrypt information and preserve two of main security principles: confidentiality and integrity. So far, the machine learning for this work cannot effiently handle with a large input (i.e. greate than a hundred bits), is vulnerable to any variat of adversarial attak and does not guarantee that it can fully reproduce the information from their ciphertexts.

Incomplete-Input Adversary: Adversarial Attack with Incomplete Input

Pablo Rivas and Korn Sooksatra
Detail

Although deep learning can show the potential use in many appliactions in the real world such as self-driving cars, it is still substantially vulnarable to adversarial attacks. These adversarial attacks craft small perturbations and add them to a natural input to deviate decisions made by deep learning. Our study is to discover if there is any effect on the performance of adversarial attacks if such attacks cannot access the whole and find the reason behind those effects if they exist.
